About Food Effects

Medical conditions or health concerns are looked at either as an undesirable condition that you want to heal or control its symptoms (like the flu) or a desirable condition that you want to reach or maintain (like heart health). To help you understand the impact of foods on these conditions, this application uses a variety of icons to illustrate the effects.

indicates the food supports a positive condition or helps control an undesirable one.
indicates the opposite, having a harmful impact on the condition
indicates that the food may not have a beneficial chemical in itself but helps the body absorb or process it.
indicates foods that inhibit absorption of a beneficial chemical.
indicates opposing impacts that is often due to different chemicals in the same food or different impacts if the food is consumed in different forms or quantities.

Some examples:
Grapefruits (vitamin C) enhance absorption of iron from other foods, and therefore would show for iron deficiency (anemia).
Beets on the otherhand have oxalates that interfere with the absorption of calcium, and would show for bones.

Vitamins & Minerals

Vitamins and minerals may be searched in two different ways; as a health food (supplement) or as a medical condition like deficiency of that vitamin or mineral.
