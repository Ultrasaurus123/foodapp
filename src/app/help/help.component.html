About Food Effects

Medical conditions or health concerns are looked at either as an undesirable condition that you want to heal or control its symptoms (like the flu) or a desirable condition that you want to reach or maintain (like heart health). To help you understand the impact of foods on these conditions, this application uses a variety of icons to illustrate the effects.

indicates the food or remedy supports a positive health condition or helps control an undesirable one.

indicates that the food may not have a beneficial chemical in itself but helps the body absorb or process it.

indicates the opposite, having a harmful impact on the health condition.

indicates foods that inhibit absorption of a beneficial chemical.

indicates opposing impacts that is often due to different chemicals in the same food or different impacts if the food is consumed in different forms or quantities.

Some examples:
Grapefruits (vitamin C) enhance absorption of iron from other foods, and therefore would show for iron deficiency (anemia).
Beets on the otherhand have oxalates that interfere with the absorption of calcium, and would show for bones.

These icons do not differentiate between preventive or curing health foods. To find out the exact impact click on the icon to view the detailed explanation page.

Control Features

Clicking on any icon will provide a brief explanation of that “effect” with live links to cited sources along with some Google search images.
Clicking on one or more row titles will select them to be hidden (use menu button).
Clicking on one or more column titles will hide all rows that do not have any impact (icon) on selected columns. To unhide the rows Click on the column titles again.
Clicking on a health condition in the Other Considerations list on the Explanation page will add/remove it to the Matrix.

Vitamins and Minerals

Vitamins and minerals may be searched in two different ways; as a health food (supplement) or as a medical condition like deficiency of that vitamin or mineral.

Health Foods and More

While the majority of the health foods and remedies in this application are edible herbs or foods, the database does include some items that are intended for external use only. Please refer to the explanation page and their cited references for proper application of these items.
